Common Divisors and Common Multiples

Let a and b be integers that are not both equal to 0. The greatest common divisor (gcd) of a and b is the largest d ∈ N for which d | a and b d | b. In symbols, we have d = gcd (a, b).

Example:

Find the greatest common divisor of 18 and 30

Sol'n
The divisors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, and 18
The divisors of 30: 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 15, 30
The gcd (18, 30) = 6

Relatively Prime

Let a, b ∈ Z. We say a and b are relatively prime if gcd (a, b) = 1.

Let p be prime, and n be an integer. If p doest not divide n, then gcd (p, n) = 1.

Examples:

77 and 250

21 and 22

12 and 13

Common Multiple

If two integers each divide a number m, then m is called a common multiple of these integers.

Let a and b be nonzero integers. The least common multiple (LCM) of a and b is the smallest m ∈ N for which a | m and b | m. In symbols,

m = lcm (a, b).

Examples:

Find the least common multiple of each pairs of integers.

  • lcm (1960, 1100)
    1960 = 2^3 * 5^1 * 7^2
    1100 = 2^2 * 5^2 * 11^1
           2^3 * 5^2 * 7^2 * 11 = 107,800

  • lcm (27,85)
    27 = 3^3
    85 = 5 * 17
           3^3 * 5 * 17 = 2295

The Division Theorem

Let a ∈ Z and d ∈ N. Then there exist unique integers q and r such that a = dq + r and 0 ≤ r < d.

Examples:

  • Divide 329 by 67
    329 = 4 * 67 + 61

  • Divide -120 by 50
    -120 = -3 * 50 + 30
